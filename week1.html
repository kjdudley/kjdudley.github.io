What is permaculture?

  • Global context (world view) ← social as well as ecological.
  • Inner landscape (inside ourselves) → we have a choice, e.g. plant more trees.

Permaculture is the design response to address problems with the world.

  • Resilient
  • Regenerative
  • Abundant

Disconnect is the root cause of problems. Permaculture is about reconnecting. Design never ends. Change is the only constant thing.

The twelve Principles of Permaculture

From the co-founder of permaculture, David Holmgren's Permaculture: Principles and Pathways Beyond Sustainability. These are not rules telling us what to do; they are guidelines to tell us what to think about when we are deciding what to do.

  1. Observe and Interact. Experimentation. A failure is a first attempt at learning. We have to learn and adapt.
  2. Obtain a yield. Take - produce/food. Aim to get 5 things back for everything put in.
  3. Catch and store energy. Future proofing.
  4. Apply self-regulation and accept feedback.
  5. Use and value renewable resources and services.
